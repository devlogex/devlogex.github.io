---
title: "Learning a Language Through Reading Is Like Playing a Puzzle Game"
description: "A perspective on how reading in a foreign language works best when we focus on meaning, not translation"
pubDate: 2026-03-24
tags: ["language", "reading", "learning"]
---

I believe that learning a language through reading is similar to playing a puzzle game.

When we read an English book, we should not try to translate word by word or understand every sentence exactly. Doing so can be distracting, and we may lose focus on the story. It also doesn't help improve our language skills effectively.

Instead, our goal should be to understand the meaning of each section. These sections can be big or small, but each one carries a main idea. What we need to do is grasp that idea and connect it with others to understand the overall message of the book.

Within each section, phrases and groups of words are like puzzle pieces. Our task is to arrange these pieces in a logical way to understand the meaning of the section.

In this way, reading a book becomes like playing a puzzle game. It becomes much simpler when we understand this approach and learn to enjoy the process.
